\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
\begin{small}

Bacteriophages are viruses that infect bacteria, showing promise as alternatives to antibiotics in phage therapy. Accurate classification of phage lifestyle - virulent or temperate - is essential for therapeutic applications, as only virulent phages are safe for use. Traditional laboratory-based methods are time-consuming and inapplicable to the majority of unculturable phages. Existing computational tools such as PhaTYP and DeePhage still face limitations when processing short contigs from metagenomic data.

This thesis proposes PhaBERT-CNN, a novel hybrid deep learning architecture that combines the DNABERT-2 genome foundation model with multi-scale convolutional neural networks and attention pooling mechanism. Unlike PhaTYP which relies on protein prediction, PhaBERT-CNN operates directly on raw DNA sequences, overcoming limitations when processing short contigs. The model leverages pre-trained representations from DNABERT-2, uses parallel CNN branches with kernel sizes 3, 5, and 7 to extract multi-scale features, and applies self-attention mechanism to focus on highly informative genomic regions.

Experiments on four contig length groups (100-400 bp, 400-800 bp, 800-1200 bp, 1200-1800 bp) with 5-fold cross-validation demonstrate that PhaBERT-CNN achieves accuracies of 81.59\%, 87.91\%, 90.01\%, and 90.69\% respectively, improving 0.91-5.98\% over state-of-the-art methods including PhaTYP, DeePhage, and ProkBERT. The model maintains a good balance between sensitivity (82.00\%-91.12\%) and specificity (80.15\%-90.95\%) across all length ranges.

The research results demonstrate the effectiveness of combining genome foundation models with task-specific architectures, providing a promising paradigm for biological sequence analysis tasks, particularly in metagenomic analysis with incomplete sequences.
