\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
\begin{small}

Bacteriophages are viruses that infect bacteria, showing promise as alternatives to antibiotics in phage therapy. Accurate classification of phage lifestyle --- virulent or temperate --- is essential for therapeutic applications, as only virulent phages are safe for clinical use; temperate phages risk lysogenic conversion and horizontal transfer of virulence genes. Traditional laboratory-based methods are time-consuming and inapplicable to the majority of unculturable phages. Existing computational tools such as PhaTYP and DeePhage depend on gene prediction tools (Prodigal), which fail on short contigs from metagenomic data, while k-mer-based approaches suffer from information leakage.

This thesis proposes PhaBERT, a novel hybrid deep learning architecture that combines the DNABERT-2 genome foundation model with a multi-kernel convolution (MKC) module consisting of multi-scale convolutional neural network branches and masked mean pooling. Unlike PhaTYP which relies on protein prediction, PhaBERT operates directly on raw DNA sequences, overcoming limitations when processing short contigs. The model leverages pre-trained representations from DNABERT-2, uses parallel CNN branches with kernel sizes 3, 5, and 7 to extract multi-scale features, and applies masked mean pooling to synthesize global representations from all sequence positions.

Experiments on four contig length groups (100-400 bp, 400-800 bp, 800-1200 bp, 1200-1800 bp) with 5-fold cross-validation demonstrate that PhaBERT achieves accuracies of 82.26\%, 87.38\%, 90.01\%, and 91.34\% respectively, improving 0.32--6.61\% over state-of-the-art methods including PhaTYP, DeePhage, and ProkBERT. The model maintains a good balance between sensitivity (82.54\%--92.31\%) and specificity (81.30\%--87.71\%) across all length ranges.

These results demonstrate the effectiveness of combining a genome foundation model with a task-specific architecture, particularly when operating directly on raw DNA sequences without gene prediction dependency. This represents a promising paradigm for biological sequence analysis tasks, especially metagenomic analysis involving incomplete or fragmented sequences.
