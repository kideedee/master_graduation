\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
\begin{small}

Reconstructing accurate 3D point clouds of telecommunication antenna towers from 2D images plays a pivotal role in enabling automated management of devices and telecom infrastructure, thereby improving precision and reducing operational costs. This thesis proposes an enhanced solution called Hybrid-SfM, which combines modern deep learning-based feature extraction and matching techniques, such as Aliked \cite{zhao2023aliked} and LightGlue \cite{lindenberger2023LightGlue}—with the traditional SIFT algorithm \cite{Lowe04Distinctive} within the Structure from Motion (SfM) pipeline. The goal is to improve the accuracy of camera pose estimation under real-world and challenging conditions, such as smooth, monochromatic surfaces and complex outdoor lighting. Additionally, the thesis assesses the impact of the proposed method on the reconstruction of detailed and dense 3D point clouds using two popular approaches: Multi-View Stereo (MVS) \cite{schoenberger2016mvs} and Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) \cite{Mildenhall2020NeRF}. Experiments on five real-world datasets collected from telecom sites demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method and offer comparative analyses of the advantages and limitations of MVS and NeRF for this specific application domain.

\vspace*{1cm}
\textbf{Keywords}: \textit{Camera pose estimation}, \textit{3D point cloud reconstruction}, \textit{Structure from Motion}, \textit{Neural Radiance Fields}, \textit{Wireless device management}, \textit{Unmanned Aerial Vehicle}
\end{small}
